Canvas Kingdoms

About

You’re a princess, and you’re done waiting for a prince to come rescue you. You’ve been lost in your paintings for far too long. But it’s a long journey out… will you be able to make it?

Project Info

👥 4 | ⏱ 7 weeks | 🧠 Unity (C#)

Introduction

This game has fairly standard platformer controls. The player will enter paintings and be transported to the worlds they see depicted. From there, they must jump across a series of platforms and obstacles to collect lost paintbrushes, pigments, or pieces of her own artwork. The princess uses what she finds to complete paintings, revealing new areas in the castle, maybe even a way out!

3D Platformer Character Controller

Walking

Players can move through the world using W / A / S / D.

High Jump

Press jump while crouched to perform a super jump.

Variable Jump Height

Holding the jump button longer results in a higher jump.

Crouch

Press Ctrl to crouch, reducing the player's height to dodge hazards and move under obstacles.

Long Jump

While moving, crouch and jump at the same time to gain a burst of directional movement.

Animation, SFX & VFX

All actions are animated with sound effects. VFX are colorful paint bursts to make movement feel responsive and fair.

Drop Shadow

A shadow directly below the player helps gauge where they will land.

Coyote Time & Jump Buffering

Coyote time: allow a short window to jump after leaving a platform. Jump buffering: queue a jump pressed slightly before landing to execute on touchdown.

Terminal Velocity

Max horizontal/vertical speeds vary by state (falling > jumping > walking/sprinting).

Design Overview

Inspired by disney movies and old cgi barbie movies, players will explore fantastical fantasy locations, each inspired by iconic fairy tale stories. The castle is dark and oppressive, while the paintings are vibrant and surreal, creating a stark contrast. Each painting is a unique, self-contained world with its own art style, physics, and platforming challenges

What I Learned

This game was my first unity game, as well as my first game using 3D! I spent alot of time getting to know how the unity physics engine works, as well as how to take some control back from it for satisfying gameplay. during development my goal was to make my code as reusable as possible, which taught me a new way of thinking while I was developing the game
